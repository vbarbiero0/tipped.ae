It surprises almost everyone: the UAE gets cold. Not Scandinavian cold — but desert climates lose their heat fast after sunset, and on January nights the temperature can drop to 10°C, sometimes lower inland, with wind and damp besides. For a street cat who spent the summer surviving heat, winter brings the opposite problem: staying warm through long, chilly nights on cold concrete.

The kind news is that winter is the easier season to help with. A little shelter goes a very long way.

## What a winter shelter needs

**Small is warm.** A cat heats a small space with their own body. A shelter the size of a large storage box is right for one or two cats; a big open crate stays as cold as the night air.

**Raised off the ground.** Concrete and tiles pull heat out of a body all night. A few centimetres of lift — bricks, a wooden pallet, even a thick board — makes an enormous difference.

**A doorway, not an open side.** A small entrance hole (about 15 cm) keeps wind out and warmth in, and lets the cat feel safe. If you can, face it away from the prevailing wind and towards a wall.

**Straw or fleece inside — never towels or folded blankets.** It sounds backwards, but towels and blankets absorb damp and then hold the cold against the cat. Straw traps warm air and stays dry; a fleece layer is a fine substitute. Whatever you use, check it monthly and swap it if it's damp.

## Build it so summer can't turn it into a trap

Here's the part that matters in this climate: **a shelter that's perfect in January can be dangerous in July.** Insulated, sealed boxes hold heat exactly as well as they hold warmth, and a cat sheltering in one during a hot spell can overheat.

The simple fix is to build for winter and open up for summer:

- Choose a design with a **removable lid or an extra panel you can take off** when the weather warms.
- Skip permanent insulation like foam-lined walls; rely on **small size + straw** instead, both of which you can undo in April.
- In summer, the same raised base becomes a shaded, airy platform — take the walls off and it keeps serving.

A plastic storage box with a hole cut in one side, set on bricks, with straw inside, ticks every box — and costs almost nothing.

## Where to place it

Somewhere quiet, sheltered from wind and rain, away from foot traffic, and — importantly — somewhere you have permission to leave it. A corner of a car park, behind a building near where the cats already gather. Cats accept shelters fastest when they're placed along paths they already use.

## Know the signs a cat is struggling with cold

- Shivering, or a cat hunched tight with fur puffed for hours
- Lethargy or unusual stillness on a cold morning
- Ears or paws that feel cold and look pale

A cat showing these signs — especially a kitten or an elderly cat — needs warmth right away and **a vet visit if they don't perk up quickly** once warm.

Winter here is short. A shelter you build one weekend in November keeps a cat comfortable through the whole of it, year after year.

*One storage box, one hole, some straw — this weekend's small project could be someone's warm winter.*
